I am a first-year M.S. student in Robotics at the GRASP Lab at the University of Pennsylvania. I'm broadly interested in machine learning and robotics, and I hope to build intelligent agents that can generalize to a wide range of real-world scenarios.

Previously, I received my B.S. in Computer Science from Virginia Tech. As an undergraduate, I was a research assistant at the Sanghani Center for Artificial Intelligence and Data Analytics, where I was advised by Naren Ramakrishnan. After graduating, I spent some time working on computer vision techniques for monocular dense reconstruction at MathWorks.

I am currently seeking research and internship opportunities in computer vision, machine learning, human-robot interaction, and robotics. Feel free to reach out to me at jkatyan [at] upenn [dot] edu.

Personal Projects
IMC Prosperity 3

Three friends and I competed in the 2025 IMC Prosperity Global Trading Competition, the "world's most elaborate trading challenge." Our team, the Hustling Hokies, placed top 15 in the United States and ranked in the top 0.1% out of 12,000+ teams worldwide.

SafeSound

Three friends and I built SafeSound, a real-time audio augmentation tool that uses natural language processing to intelligently filter trauma-inducing language during conversation. Our project won Best Overall at HackViolet 2024, a hackathon hosted at Virginia Tech.

NanoBrick

In collaboration with students from SUPSI and Alamein International University, our team developed NanoBrick, a mobile application that leverages lightweight segmentation and classification algorithms to efficiently verify LEGO kit contents on resource-constrained devices. Our project won First Place at VTURCS 2024, Virginia Tech's Undergraduate Research in Computer Science symposium.
